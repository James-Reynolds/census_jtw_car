Bayside census analysis

Working note

Dr James Reynolds

2024-06-14

Introduction

The Public Transport Research Group (PTRG) is reviewing census data for 2011, 2016 and 2021 for Greater Melbourne and the City of Bayside. Journey to work travel, mode and total cars per person are the particular areas of interest.

This working note details the analysis and results.

Methodology

Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) census data was obtained using the TableBuilder platform. Details are as follows

Population and population over 18

Population of driving age was extracted from the census results that are enumerated by place of usual residence. Data was loaded and cleaned for 2011, 2016 and 2021.

Cars per dwelling

Work and journey-to-work status on census day

Around 44k people resident in Bayside worked on census day in 2021, up from around 39k in 2011 . The share of the population working increased from 42.1% to 43.8%, as did the share of the population who were employed but did not work on the day of the census itself (from 4.9% to 5.8%). Changes in the share of the population working, not working and not employed in Bayside are generally similar to those for the rest of Melbourne.

Pre-COVID, Baysides journey-to-work transit mode share was up more (15.5% in 2011 to 18.2% in 2016) than rest of Melbourne (15.6% to 17.3%). In 2021, Bayside has a lower transit (3.8%) and higher WFH share (48.9%) than the rest of Melbourne (6.1% and 32.3%).

During COVID there were large shifts to WFH in both Bayside (49%) and the rest of Melbourne (32%). Transit use dropped to 4% and 6%, while driving also dropped to 41% and 54% respectively.

Discussion and Conclusions

Findings from this review of census data for 2011, 2016 and 2021 for the City of Bayside and the rest of Greater Melbourne include:

Population:

  • The City of Bayside makes up around 2% of the population of Greater Melbourne.
  • There were around 10,000 more people living in Bayside in 2021 compared to 2011 (+10%).
  • This growth rate is less than that of Greater Melbourne as a whole (+23%).
  • Around 79K people in Bayside in 2021 were aged 18 years or older (78.3%). This is a similar share to the rest of Greater Melbourne (78.5%).

Private vehicles4 Parked at or near dwellings on census night. Not including trucks or bicycles.:

  • Bayside also has around 2% of the private vehicles in Greater Melbourne.
  • There were around 11k more private vehicles in Bayside in 2021 than 2011.
  • This growth rate (+20%) was less than that for the rest of Greater Melbourne (+31%).
  • There were 0.861 vehicles per person aged 18 or older in 2021 in Bayside.
  • This was 7% higher than the rate for the rest of Greater Melbourne (0.805).
  • This was also 5.9% higher than the rate in Bayside in 2011 (0.813), whereas the rest of Greater Melbourne only grew by 5.9% (from 0.763).

Working status:

  • ~44k Bayside residents worked on census day in 2021 (43.8%), ~6k were employed by not working (5.8%)5 A small number were eligible to, but did not respond to the JTW question (196, 0.2%), and so are not included in the other categories. and ~51k (50.2%) were not in the labor force. This share is generally similar to the rest of Greater Melbourne.
  • There were around 6k more people living in Bayside who worked on census day in 2021 than in 2011 (+15%), but this rate of increase is less than the rest of Greater Melbourne (+26%)

Journey-to-work and WFH mode/activity share:

  • Transit use had increasing pre-COVID in Bayside, from 15% in 2011 to 18.2% in 2016.
  • This increase was higher than for the rest of Greater Melbourne (15.6% to 17.3%)
  • Driving a private vehicle had dropped in Bayside from 68% in 2011 to 64.8% in 2016, both of which were lower than the rest of Greater Melbourne (69.8% dropping to 68.2%).
  • COVID-19 induced a large shift from transit (3.8% in Bayside in 2021) and driving (41.2%) to WFH (up from 8.7% in 2016 to 48.9% in 2021).
  • The shift to WFH was more pronounced in Bayside than the rest of Greater Melbourne, which was 6.1% transit, 54% drive and 32.3% WFH in 2021.
  • For those travelling to work, there was a shift from transit to driving. In Bayside transit share dropped from 20% in 2016 to 7% in 2021, while driving increased from 71% to 81%. These changes were similar to the rest of Greater Melbourne6 Transit 18% in 2016 to 9% in 2021. Driving 71% in 2016 to 80% in 2021..
  • Among transit users there was a shift from train7 Bayside 93% in 2016 to 86% in 2021, rest of Melbourne 73% to 67% to bus8 4% to 11% for Bayside, and 10% to 16% for the rest of Greater Melbourne.

Overall

  • Bayside has been growing in people and vehicles, but at lower rates than the rest of Greater Melbourne.
  • Transit mode share was going up, but COVID led to a very large shift to WFH, which is larger in Bayside than the rest of Greater Melbourne.
  • Transit (especially train) has lost share to driving, but the number of people driving to work has still decreased by around 9k9 Down 32% from ~27k to ~18k in Bayside, compared to a fall of 10% for the rest of Greater Melbourne while transit JTW ridership has fallen only around 6k10 Down 78% from ~8k to ~2k, compared to a fall of 60% for the rest of Greater Melbourne.

Overall, in comparison to the rest of Greater Melbourne, Bayside had: lower growth in population and vehicles; greater drops in JTW transit ridership and driving; but more vehicles per person aged 18 or older and more WFH.
